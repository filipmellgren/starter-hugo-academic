Model

Labor market

  • \(L\) workers.
  • Two skill types: skilled (\(s\)) and unskilled (\(u\)).
  • Monopsonistic.
    • Markdown: \(\lambda\).

Firm entry

  • Fixed cost to enter and learn \(\Omega_i\), TFP.
    • Ex ante source of firm heterogeneity.

Product market

  • Monopolistic sectors \(j \in J\) with continuum \(n_j\) firms.
    • Ignore \(j\)-subscript.
  • Horizontally differentiated varieties.
  • Inverse demand as in Klenow and Willis (2016).
    • Absolute demand elasticities decrease with size.
  • Optimal markups:

\[\color{salmon}{\mu_i} = \frac{\bar{\sigma}}{\bar{\sigma} - \left( \frac{Y_{i}^{*}}{Y^*}\right)^{\frac{\epsilon}{\bar{\sigma}}}}\]

Production technology

\[y_i = \Omega_i \left( \color{darkcyan}{E_i} l_{s,i}^{\frac{\rho-1}{\rho}} + l_{u,i}^{\frac{\rho - 1}{\rho}} \right)^{\frac{\rho}{\rho - 1}}\]

  • Fixed production costs \(f(E_i)\), convex in \(E_i\).
    • Example: software, skill-specific capital, management practices.
  • Firms choose heterogenous \(E_i\), inducing skill-firm sorting.
    • Skill premium disproportionately higher in firms with high \(E_i\).

Final demand

  • Competitive final numeraire good with Kimball demand:

\[\int_{i \in j} \mathcal{Y}\bigg( \frac{y_i}{Y} \bigg) di = 1\]

  • \(\mathcal{Y}\) is upper incomplete Gamma function as in Klenow and Willis (2016),

Firm problem, step 1

Choose employment, \(l_s\), \(l_u\), conditional on \(E_i\):

\[\max_{l_{s,i}, l_{u,i}} p_i y_i - w_{s, i}l_{s,i} - w_{u,i}l_{u,i}\]

subject to demand and production technology.

Firm problem, step 2

Choose \(E_i\), taking as given optimal input conditional on \(E_i\):

\[\max_{E_i^*} R_i^{**} \cdot \left(1 - \frac{\lambda}{\mu_i^{**}} \right) - f(E_i^*)\]

  • \(f(E_i^*)\) is a fixed cost, convex in \(E_i^*\).

Optimal skill technology adoption

Characterized by:

\[\left(1 - \frac{1}{\color{salmon}{\mu_i^{**}}} \right) \frac{d \log R_i^{**}}{d \log \color{darkcyan}{E_i^*}} +\left( \frac{1}{\color{salmon}{\mu_i^{**}}}\right) \frac{d \log \color{salmon}{\mu_i^{**}}}{d \log \color{darkcyan}{E_i^*}} =\frac{f'(\color{darkcyan}{E_i^*})\color{darkcyan}{E_i^*}}{R_i^{**}}\]

\(\rightarrow\) High markup firms care more about scale than margins.

Equilibrium labor shares

Optimal labor demand

  • \(\frac{w_{u,i} l_{u,i}}{p_i y_i} = \color{salmon}{\mu_i}^{-1} \left( 1 - \frac{1}{1 + \frac{1}{\color{darkcyan}{E_i}} \left(\frac{w_{s,i}}{w_{u,i}}\right)^{\rho - 1}} \right)\)

  • \(\frac{w_{s,i} l_{s,i}}{p_i y_i} = \color{salmon}{\mu_i}^{-1} \frac{1}{1 + \frac{1}{\color{darkcyan}{E_i}} \left(\frac{w_{s,i}}{w_{u,i}}\right)^{\rho - 1}}\)

\(\rightarrow\) Markups depress the unskilled labor share.
